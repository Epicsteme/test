This artwork was inspired by the quote "If you judge a fish by its ability to climb a tree, it will live its whole life believing that it is stupid." Here, the fish is not trying to climb a tree—it is the tree. The idea is that you should not judge yourself based on someone else's standards but by your own. You are your own standard. You are the learner and the standard.

This artwork was inspired by the story of a blind man who carries a lamp at night—not to see, but so others can see him. He borrows the idea from a friend, thinking it will keep him safe. However, as he walks home, someone bumps into him. The blind man says "Can't you see the lamp?" and the other man says “Your lamp isn't lit.” The lantern containing the moon is a symbol of borrowed wisdom. The moon represents wisdom, much like how certain nocturnal creatures use it to navigate. For them, the moon is a fixed point—a dependable guide in darkness. Similarly, wisdom serves as a reference system for those navigating life. This artwork challenges the idea of relying on secondhand wisdom.

The teacup represents the mind, and the clouds above symbolize the flow of ideas and opportunities. Some clouds bring rain—valuable new knowledge or perspectives. However, the cup is already full. Unless it's emptied with intention, it cannot receive more. Letting go is an essential part of growth. Just like in chess, where you may sacrifice a piece to win the larger game, the mind must release old thoughts, beliefs, or assumptions at the right time to make room for something better. The value is in knowing what to hold, what to release, and when.
