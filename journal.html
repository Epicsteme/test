Teacher Problem

This is not an act of discrimination, defamation, or false claims- these are facts and the truth needs to be acknowledged. The purpose of this post is to spread awareness of the cruelties and injustice that took place in schools and colleges in at least some regions in the near past; and to help justify my state of health (This post is also a bit of cry for help) I was never against learning nor did I ever act in ways that could be considered wrong by any healthy adult. I have studied in many schools in many places since my parent's job required them to move around often. Most of my teachers made me feel unsafe and unmotivated. I hold these people responsible for my failures... not my failures- they have always been my teachers' failures. Note that not all teachers are incompetent - some teachers take their responsibilities seriously and their commitment should be acknowledged and appreciated.

One of the primary schools I have been to was St Johns EM School in Tenali. Some of the teachers that I had back then lacked the interpersonal skills necessary to teach a child in a healthy manner which could allow the child to grow up to be a healthy individual. One of the teachers had another student spank me because I did not know the answer to a question. Another teacher had me kneel down for not getting enough marks in an exam, and when a student pointed out that a river ran down my face, she said "let him cry." These were only a few of many abusive incidents I faced when I was a child.

I've also been to Howard Model School in Hyderabad. Most of the teachers I had were mostly abusive both orally and physically. A teacher bashed me vigorously with a cane because I wrote "PTO" a couple of times on an empty page (as opposed to writing only once).

Part of my highschooling was done in All Saints High School in Hyderabad. A teacher bashed me vigorously with a cane one day and I still do not know why (I assume she did not like my handwriting since she initiated the act when I showed her my notes) Another teacher said to my face "why can't you just be normal?" because I was irregular to school (which was so because I felt unsafe in the school) She also said to me in front of the whole class "Pregnant women get vomitings!" when I was late to class due to sickness. Another teacher broke my plastic scale mid-exam because a student borrowed it instead of bringing his own scale.

I completed high school in JMJ EM School in Anakapalle. I finally snapped here when a teacher was being unreasonable and abusive. After being enlightened regarding my sincerity by my parents, the teachers there have learned to be a little more reasonable with me.

I can't say too much about higher secondary school since they already had an incident with another student just before I joined the school. He stabbed a teacher and she died of blood loss. They did try to be careful with me due to their past experience (St Marys AIHS School).

As for my first Bachelor's Degree, I went to Madras Christian College. I learned some of the most important lessons regarding the social aspects of our culturally diverse society from the professors and the students. Some of the professors had unhealthy habits as part of their teaching methods- I thought they were trying to teach me to become a better version of myself (as is recommended by the National Education Policy of India) but they were imposing their own beliefs and expecting me to act more like them. Due to some of the western influences, there was not enough empathy and sense of unity in some of the professors, and they failed to act based on the truth that we are all on the same side. They were also rude to me on more than one occassion without realising that I did something as a result of their incompetencies.

I hope that the future teachers take their responsibilities more seriously than those from the past, and that a sense of unity will be promoted in order to create a more empathetic society; that the unhealthy influences of the British colonials will soon be replaced by the productive principles of the East.
